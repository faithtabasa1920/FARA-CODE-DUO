Lily

Lilies are known for their large, showy, and often fragrant flowers, typically with six petals and six stamens, and grow from bulbs. They come in a wide variety of colors and shapes, including trumpet, funnel, cup, bell, bowl, or flat forms.

Violet

Violets, belonging to the genus Viola, are small, herbaceous perennial plants with heart-shaped or rounded leaves and typically five-petaled flowers, often in shades of purple, blue, white, or yellow, and are known for their delicate fragrance.

Learn More

Lotus

The lotus flower is an aquatic perennial with large, flat leaves that float on the surface of water, and it's known for its large, round flowers with many petals, often seen in shades of pink or white, and is revered for its symbolism of purity, rebirth, and enlightenment

Lilac

Lilacs are fragrant, spring-flowering shrubs or small trees with clusters of small, four-petaled flowers, typically in shades of purple, but also white, pink, blue, and even yellow, with a sweet, often described as rose-like, scent.

Snowdrop

Snowdrops (Galanthus nivalis) are small, bulbous perennials with dainty, nodding white flowers that emerge in early spring, often before the snow melts, and feature six petal-like segments (tepals) with green markings on the inner ones.

Orchid

Orchid flowers are known for their unique and diverse forms, often featuring three sepals, three petals (one modified into a "lip" or labellum), and a central column structure, with the labellum playing a key role in attracting pollinators.

Buttercup

Buttercups are plants in the genus Ranunculus, known for their bright yellow, glossy flowers and deeply cut or lobed leaves, and are often found in meadows and pastures.

Cornation

Carnations, scientifically known as Dianthus caryophyllus, are herbaceous perennial plants with fragrant, ruffled petals in a wide array of colors, often used in bouquets and corsages.
